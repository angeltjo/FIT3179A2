Gender Inequality In...

Global Population

This map shows the Global Gender Equality Score for countries
all over the world. The higher the score is, the
more gender equal the country is. From the map, we
can see that the most gender equal country is Iceland
meaning the number of females to males is more even
than other countries. The least gender equal country is Yemen.

This graph shows the global distribution of females and males by ages. In the younger ages, there are more males than females however in the older age ranges, there are more females than males. All together, there is a greater global population of males than females.

Economy

This graph shows the gender wage gap and the percentage of women in the Top 5% for certain countries. To be in the Top 5%, you must have an average annual wage of $342,987. Norway has the lowest share of women in the top 5% while also having a lower gender wage gap.

The graph below shows the gender wage gap over the
years for some countries. South Korea has the highest gender
wage gap, while Sweden and Australia have the lowest gender
wage gaps out of the countries included.

Education

These graphs show the percentage of each cohort who are
three to five years older than the intended last age
of each level of education have completed it globally. The
levels of education are primary, lowever secondary and upper secondary.

Primary School Completion Rate

Primary school is usually done at the ages of
6 to 11 years old. From the graphs, we can see
that 82% of females completed primary school and 82% of
males completed primary school. While it is the same percentage,
note that in our population distribution graph that we found
for this age range there are more boys than girls.

Lower Secondary School Completion Rate

Lower Secondary school is usually taken from the ages of
11 to 15 years old.

Upper Secondary School Completion Rate

Upper Secondary school is usually taken from the ages of
16 to 18 years old.

Workplace

The graph below shows the percentage composition of genders in
roles in the workplace. We can see that for all
managerial positions, there are more males than females. There is also a
large difference in the percentage of men and women working
as Machinery operators and drivers and technicians and trades,
which are overwhemingly male dominated.
